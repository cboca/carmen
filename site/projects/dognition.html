Dognition

http://www.dognition.com

We started pretty much from scratch on the Dognition product. The first tasks at hand were to start to create a customer journey, develop target personas, concept the product phases, and start developing the brand.

This concept model was something I started early in the concepting phase and continued to update as we collaborated through the product development.

As we developed our target personas, brand strategy, and started to work on our alpha version of the online product, the visual designer, dev team and I worked together and sketched out our ideas. My desk and walls are usually covered in sketches and hacked together paper prototypes.

I led user research throughout the alpha, beta, and live phases. At the same time as we were learning from this testing of content, functionality, and experience, I was creating the logic flows for the experience, the sitemap, and functional specs and wireframes. The tight-knit team worked together daily to review, question, concept, and create content around these documents.

Working on Dognition was a unique mix of constantly testing and optimizing of the marketing site, online product, subscription service, and marketing/social outreach. I also worked on concepting of new features and future phases.
